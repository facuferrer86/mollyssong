**SCENE 1 — OPENING VOICEOVER WITH ADULT MOLLY (MACHINE MEMORY)**

**BLACK SCREEN.**

A woman's voice, calm and reflective — a consciousness speaking from beyond flesh and bone.

**MOLLY (V.O.)**
I remember my father. The warmth of his coat in winter. The scratch of his stubble against my cheek. The way his fingers smelled like metal and dust.

He used to hum when he worked. Three notes. Always the same. I'd pretend to be asleep, just to hear him do it.

He was a man who loved me. A man who protected me. A man who betrayed me. He was a man I'll never forget.

**FADE IN:**

**INT. UNDERGROUND LAB - NIGHT**

A single bulb swings above, casting soft amber light.

Old machines sleep quietly. Tools line the tables. The room is full of wires, cables, notes pinned to walls — a kind of organized survival.

ELIAS QUINN (40s) walks barefoot through the lab, carrying MOLLY (2) in his arms. She sleeps against his shoulder, face nestled into the collar of his coat.

He hums a simple tune. Three notes. A lullaby built from love and fear.

He sits in a cracked leather chair and holds her tighter. Closes his eyes. The hum continues, barely above a whisper.

**MOLLY (V.O.)**
He told me stories. About the oceans. About the moon. About the world that used to be. I didn't care if they were true.

I just wanted to hear his voice.

The room is still. The hum of machines rises beneath the melody.

**MOLLY (V.O.)**
This is where it began. In a room full of broken things, with a melody I can still remember.

Even now.
