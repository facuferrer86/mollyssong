**INT. UNDERGROUND LAB - DAY**

Sunlight, heavily filtered, struggles through a grimy skylight high above.

The lab is in its usual state of organized chaos.

MOLLY (12) sits cross-legged on a stool at a cluttered workbench, her focus absolute. She’s coaxing a small, handheld MUSIC PLAYER back to life.

She taps her fingers rhythmically, humming softly: the unmistakable riff of Elvis Presley’s "Jailhouse Rock."

The heavy METAL DOOR at the far end of the lab creaks open. ELIAS enters, coated in the grime of the outside world. He drops a heavy CANVAS BAG by the door, the sound echoing slightly.

He watches Molly for a moment, a faint, bittersweet smile on his face. He crosses to her.

<center>ELIAS</center>
> Any luck?

Molly holds up the music player. With a final twist of a small wire, a tiny, tinny speaker crackles to life, playing the first few bars of "Jailhouse Rock." Her face breaks into a triumphant grin.

<center>MOLLY</center>
> The main power coil was fused. Dead end. So I rerouted the circuit through the kinetic charger.
> (She gives it a little shake)
> It only works when it’s moving now. But it works.

<center>ELIAS</center>
> (A sad, proud smile)
> You always find a way around the broken things, don't you?

He reaches into his coat and places a small, tarnished silver LOCKET on the table. It's simple, dented, and empty.

<center>ELIAS</center>
> Found this near the old transit station.

Molly picks it up, her fingers tracing the faded engravings.

<center>MOLLY</center>
> Was it a good haul? Did you get the protein filters?

<center>ELIAS</center>
> Got them. And a new hydro-pump regulator. The old one was about to give up the ghost.

<center>MOLLY</center>
> (Quietly)
> Did you... see anyone?

Elias's expression hardens almost imperceptibly. He turns his back to her, unpacking the canvas bag.

<center>ELIAS</center>
> You see shadows out there. People fighting over scraps. It's... desperate, Molly. A kind of hunger you don't want to know. It’s better to be invisible. That's how we survive.

Molly looks down at the locket in her hand, her gaze sharp and analytical.

<center>MOLLY</center>
> A locket isn't a scrap. Someone had to lose it. Someone had to... care about it.

Elias freezes for a half-second. He still doesn't turn around.

<center>ELIAS</center>
> The world is full of ghosts. Don't go chasing them.

Molly says nothing. She just looks at his back, her expression unreadable. She deliberately reaches out and switches off the music player.

The cheerful music cuts out, plunging the room into a heavy, meaningful silence.

Elias finally turns, studying his daughter's face—the curiosity that's quickly hardening into suspicion. He sees she is no longer a child who will accept his stories without question. The walls of the lab are no longer enough.

<center>ELIAS</center>
> Tomorrow, we start something new.

Her eyes meet his, questioning.
